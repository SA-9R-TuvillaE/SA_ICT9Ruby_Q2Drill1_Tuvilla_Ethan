Emergency Go-Bag Essentials



Food & Water

Canned goods and other non-perishable foods or foods good for atleast 3 days are good for diminishing hunger.

Clean drinking water in a bottle is a must to satisfy thirst.

Clothes

Fresh and clean clothes provides warmth and protection.

Lightweight but durable and reliable clothes are recommended.

Hygiene Kit

Hygiene kits contain toiletries like shampoo, soap, toothpaste and toothbrush, etc.

This promotes clean and good health and hygiene amidst long-term emergencies.

First Aid Kit

First Aid Kits should have bandages, dressings, and other treatments for wounds.

Also promotes good health and prevents infection and disease when healthcare is limited.

Documents

Basic documents such as birth certificates and passports provide identification.

Helpful for organization of persons.

Other Tools

Flashlights, radios, extra batteries, multi-tools (like Swiss army knives), phones, chargers

Provides additional and versatile help where opportunities are limited.
